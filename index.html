About Clinical Trials

What are Clinical Trials?

Clinical trials are research studies that test new treatments, drugs, or medical approaches in people. They help doctors and scientists discover whether new treatments are safe and effective.

Comprehensive Trial Coverage

Our database searches ClinicalTrials.gov, which includes trials from:

  • MD Anderson Cancer Center - All registered trials
  • Mayo Clinic - Nationwide locations
  • Memorial Sloan Kettering - Cancer research
  • Johns Hopkins - All specialties
  • Cleveland Clinic - Major medical center
  • NCI-funded trials - National Cancer Institute
  • And 1,000+ other institutions nationwide

Why Participate?

  • Access to new treatments before they're widely available
  • Close monitoring by medical professionals
  • Contribute to medical research that helps others
  • Treatment may be provided at no cost

Understanding Eligibility

Each trial has specific requirements called inclusion and exclusion criteria. These ensure participant safety and help researchers get accurate results.

  • Eligible: You meet requirements and have no exclusions
  • Excluded: You meet some requirements but have a condition that excludes you
  • Insufficient Info: More information needed to determine eligibility

Your Rights as a Participant

  • Participation is always voluntary
  • You can withdraw at any time
  • Informed consent explains all risks and benefits
  • Your privacy is protected
  • Studies are reviewed by ethics committees (IRBs)

Frequently Asked Questions

Are clinical trials safe?

+

Clinical trials follow strict scientific and ethical guidelines. All trials must be approved by Institutional Review Boards (IRBs) that ensure participant safety. Trials progress through phases, with early phases testing safety before moving to larger studies.

Will I receive a placebo or the real treatment?

+

Some trials use placebos, while others compare new treatments to standard treatments. The informed consent document will clearly explain the study design, including the possibility of receiving a placebo. You'll know this information before deciding to participate.

How much does it cost to participate?

+

The investigational treatment and trial-related care are typically provided at no cost. Some trials may also cover travel expenses or provide compensation for your time. Your regular medical care costs may still apply and should be discussed with the research team.

How do I know if I'm eligible?

+

Each trial has specific inclusion and exclusion criteria. This search tool provides an initial match, but final eligibility is determined through screening by the research team. Contact the trial coordinator to discuss your specific situation.

Can I leave a trial after I join?

+

Yes, participation is always voluntary. You can withdraw from a clinical trial at any time for any reason. It's helpful to discuss your concerns with the research team, but the decision is ultimately yours.
